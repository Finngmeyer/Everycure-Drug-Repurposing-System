HOW IT WORKS

Three pieces of data.
One powerful idea.

The system stores three simple facts about the world, then connects the dots automatically.

STEP 01
What does each drug treat?

Every drug starts with a known, approved use — what doctors already prescribe it for.

Metformin treats Type 2 Diabetes
STEP 02
What biological targets does it affect?

Every drug works by interacting with a protein or enzyme in the body — its biological target.

Metformin affects AMPK mTOR
STEP 03
Which diseases involve those targets?

Research has shown that certain diseases occur when specific biological targets malfunction.

mTOR is linked to Cancer Aging

THE LOGIC

How matches are discovered

The system follows a simple three-hop path through a knowledge graph — the same idea that powers real AI drug discovery tools, just smaller.

  • 01 The score counts shared paths. If two targets both connect the same drug to the same disease, the score is 2. More shared targets means more independent biological evidence — a stronger signal.
  • 02 Known uses are filtered out. The system only shows matches where the drug is not already approved for that disease. If it already treats it, that is not a repurposing discovery.
  • 03 Results are ranked, not just listed. Matches are sorted from highest score to lowest. The top results are the ones with the most biological backing and are the best starting points for research.
  • 04 Adding data is the only way to expand it. The code logic never changes — only the data does. Adding a new drug means adding three lines to three dictionaries. The rest is automatic.
